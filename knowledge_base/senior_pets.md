# Senior Pet Care Guidelines

## When Is a Pet Considered Senior?

Dogs are generally considered senior at 7 years for large breeds (over 25 kg) and at 8–9 years for small and medium breeds. Cats are considered senior at 7 years and geriatric at 11+ years. Giant dog breeds such as Great Danes may be considered senior as early as 5–6 years.

## Exercise for Senior Pets

Senior dogs still need daily exercise to maintain joint health and muscle tone. However, intensity should be reduced. Replace one long walk with two or three shorter walks spread through the day. Low-impact activities such as leisurely walks and gentle swimming are ideal. Avoid running on hard surfaces, which stresses aging joints.

Senior cats benefit from short, gentle play sessions — 5–10 minutes twice daily. Soft toys and ground-level activities are preferable to high jumping, which can stress older joints.

Watch for signs that your senior pet is overdoing it: limping during or after exercise, reluctance to stand up, stiffness in the morning, or excessive fatigue after mild activity.

## Nutrition for Senior Pets

Senior pets often need fewer calories due to reduced activity, but higher-quality protein to maintain muscle mass. Look for food labeled "senior" or "mature" that is lower in calories and fat but rich in protein. Many senior pets also benefit from joint supplements containing glucosamine and chondroitin.

Fresh water should always be available. Senior pets are more prone to kidney disease and dehydration.

## Veterinary Care

Senior pets should visit the vet every six months rather than annually. Twice-yearly exams allow early detection of conditions common in older animals: arthritis, dental disease, kidney disease, hyperthyroidism (cats), diabetes, and cognitive decline. Blood work and urinalysis are often recommended annually.

## Mental Stimulation

Senior pets can experience cognitive decline similar to dementia in humans. Regular gentle mental stimulation helps slow this decline. Short training sessions (even simple commands), food puzzles, and new sniffing routes on walks all help keep aging minds active.

## Comfort and Accessibility

Older pets often benefit from orthopedic bedding that cushions aching joints. Provide ramps or steps to help them reach favorite spots without jumping. Keep food and water bowls accessible at floor level to avoid straining arthritic necks.
