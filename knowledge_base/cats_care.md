# Cat Care Guidelines

## Exercise and Play

Indoor cats need at least 15–30 minutes of active play per day to maintain a healthy weight and prevent boredom. Without adequate exercise, cats become sedentary, which increases the risk of obesity and urinary tract issues.

Interactive play sessions using wand toys, laser pointers, or feather toys are the most effective way to exercise a cat. These mimic the hunting sequence (stalk, pounce, catch) and provide both physical exercise and mental stimulation. Each session should ideally last 10–15 minutes.

Cats are most active at dawn and dusk (crepuscular). Scheduling play sessions in the morning and early evening aligns with their natural activity patterns and tends to produce better engagement.

## Daily Feeding

Adult cats (1–7 years) should be fed twice daily — morning and evening. Free-feeding dry food can lead to overeating and obesity. Measure portions according to the food's feeding guide and your cat's ideal weight.

Kittens (under 12 months) need three to four small meals per day to support rapid growth. Senior cats (7+ years) may benefit from smaller, more frequent meals if they have digestive issues.

## Litter Box Maintenance

A cat's litter box should be scooped at least once daily. A dirty litter box is a common cause of house-soiling behavior. A general rule is one litter box per cat plus one extra.

## Grooming

Short-haired cats typically groom themselves adequately and need brushing once or twice per week to reduce shedding and hairballs. Long-haired breeds such as Persians and Maine Coons need daily brushing to prevent matting and tangles.

## Veterinary Care

Adult cats should receive a wellness exam once per year. Senior cats (7+) benefit from twice-yearly exams to catch age-related conditions such as kidney disease, hyperthyroidism, and dental disease early. All cats need annual core vaccinations and regular parasite prevention.
